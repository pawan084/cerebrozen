# Active listening

**Concept.** Active listening is listening to understand rather than to reply —
giving the other person enough signal that they have been heard that they keep
talking and go deeper. It is the base skill under difficult conversations,
delegation, and coaching itself.

**The moves:**
- **Reflect, don't just nod.** Say back the gist in your own words ("so the part
  that stings is being cut out of the decision, not the decision itself?"). A good
  reflection is a hypothesis the other person can correct.
- **Name the feeling, tentatively.** "It sounds frustrating" invites more; getting
  it slightly wrong is fine — it still shows you're reaching for it.
- **Ask one more question before you solve.** The first problem stated is rarely
  the real one. "What else is going on around this?" surfaces it.
- **Leave silence.** Two seconds of quiet does more than another question.

**Anti-patterns.** Waiting for your turn to talk; jumping to advice; "at least…"
(minimising); topping their story with your own.

**Coaching cue.** When a client says "they just won't listen to me," the fastest
shift is often the client listening first — model it, then have them try it before
the next conversation.
