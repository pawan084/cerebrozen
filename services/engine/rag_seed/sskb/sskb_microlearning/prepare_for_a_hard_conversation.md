# Micro-learning: prepare for a hard conversation

A five-minute prep you can run before a conversation you're dreading.

1. **Name your goal in one sentence.** What do you want to be *true* after this
   conversation that isn't true now? If you can't say it, you're not ready.
2. **Separate the three conversations.** What happened (the facts, and where you
   might be wrong), the feelings (yours and theirs), and identity (what this makes
   you afraid it says about you). Most conversations go wrong in the second two.
3. **Open with the hard part, briefly and without a verdict.** "I want to talk
   about the deadline we missed" beats ten minutes of throat-clearing that makes
   the other person brace.
4. **Plan one genuine question.** Not a trap — a question you'd actually like the
   answer to ("How did it look from where you sat?").
5. **Decide your minimum.** The one thing you will not leave without saying.

**Reframe.** The goal is not to win the conversation; it's to still have a working
relationship *and* the truth on the table. You can be direct and kind at once —
directness is a form of respect.
